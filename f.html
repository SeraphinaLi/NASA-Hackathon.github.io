Misuse of fire by minors

Introduction:

Fires started by children under 17 have their own category. Kids aged 12 or younger may experiment with fire out of curiosity, known as "playing with matches.”



How to prevent:

1.Always supervise young children.


2.Never leave matches or lighters within children's reach. Store them in high, locked cabinets.


3.Use child-resistant lighters, but keep in mind they are not completely childproof.


4.Teach young children to immediately inform an adult if they discover matches or lighters.


If you suspect your child or adolescent is involved in fire-play, take immediate action and intervene.Begin by consulting your local fire department for guidance and assistance.Contact your local fire department; they can refer you to experts with experience in addressing juvenile firesetting, as intervention is essential.
